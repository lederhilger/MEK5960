For a moment we consider the Cartesian coördinates $x^{i}$ in $\euclidean^{d}$.
The transform to hyperspherical coördinates takes the form%
\footnote{%
  \cite{blumenson1960derivation} \textsc{Blumenson} (1960)
}

\begin{align}
  \begin{split}
    x^1 & = r \cos{\alpha^1};\\
    \vspace{2em}
    x^{j} & = r \cos{\alpha^j} \prod_{k = 1}^{j - 1} \sin{\alpha^k};\\
    \vspace{2em}
    x^{d-1} & = r \sin{\alpha^{0}} \prod_{k = 1}^{d - 2} \sin{\alpha^k};\\
    \vspace{2em}
    x^{d} & = r \cos{\alpha^{0}} \prod_{k = 1}^{d - 2} \sin{\alpha^{k}},
  \end{split}
\end{align}

\noindent
where $j \in \{ 2, \dots, d-2 \}$ for $d \geq 4$.
Nevermind the indexing, as we shall reïndex.
For three-dimensional Euclidean space, we have $\xvec = (r\cos{\alpha^1}, r\sin{\alpha^0}\sin{\alpha^1}, r\sin{\alpha^0}\cos{\alpha^1})$
