\noindent The points themselves may be given in terms of spherical coordinates, so that the distance between the points $\xvec$ and $\bzhe$ may be written in terms of the law of cosines,
\[
r = \absl{\xvec - \bzhe} = \sqrt{\absl{\xvec}^2 - 2\absl{\xvec}\absl{\bzhe}\cos{\alpha} + \absl{\bzhe}^2}.
\]
Given the above expression of the value $r$, we seek to approximate the \textsc{Green} function so that integrating it will be more efficient.
It is clear from the above that we may write
\[
\green(\xvec; \bzhe) = \frac{1}{\absl{\bzhe} \sqrt{1 + \varepsilon^2 - 2\varepsilon\cos{\alpha}}}, \quad \varepsilon = \frac{\absl{\xvec}}{\absl{\bzhe}}.
\]
Through a binomial expansion in $\varepsilon^2 - 2\varepsilon\cos{\alpha}$, and rearranging so that we have a power series in $\varepsilon$, we have that
\[
\green(\xvec; \bzhe) = \frac{1}{\absl{\bzhe}} \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \legendre_{k}(\cos{\alpha}) \varepsilon^k, \quad \varepsilon < 1,
\]
where $\legendre_{k}$ is the $k$\textsuperscript{th} \textsc{Legendre} polynomial.\footnote{\cite{abramowitz1965handbook} \textsc{Stegun}, pp.332--353}
