The boundary element method is a method of calculating values related to the fluid velocity through the second kind \textsc{Fredholm} integral equation \eqref{eq:integral_equation}, whereby the boundary is discretized into quadrilateral panels.
As has been established, the conditions under which the solution is unique is determined at the boundary, here being the \textsc{Neumann} boundary condition of impermeability,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:impermeability}
  \partial_{\nhat} \Phi(\xvec) = \nhat \cdot \Uvec, \qquad \xvec \in \partial\Omega.
\end{equation}

\noindent
We can now solve for the potential function $\Phi$ of equation \eqref{eq:Laplace_equation} by the means we see fit.
The alternative construction is to consider distributions of simple and double sources over the boundary.
The potential at the point $\xvec$ due to simple or double sources are given respectively by
\begin{subequations}\label{eq:distribution_representation}
  \begin{equation}\tag{\theequation{a ,b}}
    \Phi(\bzhe) = \int_S \frac{\sigma(\xvec)}{r} \,\dee S, \qquad \Phi(\bzhe) = \int_S \frac{\mu(\xvec) \cos{\alpha}}{r^2} \,\dee S.
  \end{equation}
\end{subequations}
This is valid for any square integrable vector field.%
\footnote{%
        \cite{byhovskij1960orthogonal} \textsc{Byhovskij} \& \textsc{Smirnov} (1960)
}\textsuperscript{,}%
\footnote{%
        \cite{morino1993boundary} \textsc{Morino} (1993)
}

Imposing the condition of impermeability of equation \eqref{eq:impermeability} on the simple pole distribution, we find an integral equation for the source distribution.
It can be shown that equation \eqref{eq:impermeability} can be recast in terms of an integral equation for te simple source distribution,
\[
  -2\pi \sigma + \int_S \sigma \partial_{\nhat} r^{-1} \,\dee S = \nhat \cdot \Uvec, \qquad \xvec \in \partial\Omega.
\]

\noindent
Confer with \textsc{Hess} \& \textsc{Smith}.%
\footnote{%
        \cite{hess1967calculation} \textsc{Hess} \& \textsc{Smith} (1967)
}
This distribution can now be inserted into equation \subeqref{\ref{eq:distribution_representation}}[a] to find the potential.
